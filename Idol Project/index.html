Description

Early Life and Education

Donald Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York City, as the fourth of five children in the Trump family. During his youth, he attended Kew-Forest School and later studied at the New York Military Academy, graduating in 1964. He then attended Fordham University before transferring to the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, where he earned a Bachelor of Science in Economics in 1968.

Career

Trump joined his father's real estate business in 1968 and became a central figure in the company's expansion during the 1970s. Over time, he developed the Trump brand across real estate, hotels, golf properties, and licensing ventures. He later became a well-known media figure through books and television, especially as host of The Apprentice (2004-2015). In politics, he was elected the 45th U.S. President in 2016 and later returned to office as the 47th U.S. President in 2025.

Core Presidential Duties

  • Leads the executive branch of the U.S. federal government.
  • Serves as commander in chief of the U.S. armed forces.
  • Signs or vetoes legislation passed by Congress.
  • Appoints cabinet members, ambassadors, and federal judges (with Senate confirmation).
  • Conducts foreign policy and represents the United States internationally.
